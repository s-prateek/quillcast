# Quillcast Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** June 28, 2026  
**Last updated:** June 28, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how **Quillcast** (“the Application,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and deletes information when you connect your LinkedIn account and use the Application to draft and publish content to LinkedIn.

Quillcast is an open-source, self-hosted content pipeline. It runs in **your own AWS account** and on **your local computer**. We do not operate a centralized service that stores your data on our servers.

---

## 1. Who This Policy Applies To

This policy applies to individuals who:

1. Install and deploy Quillcast in their own AWS environment, and  
2. Authorize Quillcast to access their LinkedIn account via LinkedIn OAuth.

In this model, **you are both the user of the Application and the person whose LinkedIn account is connected**. Quillcast is designed for single-operator, personal use. It does not provide accounts for third parties, and it does not collect data from other LinkedIn members.

---

## 2. Information We Collect from LinkedIn

When you authorize Quillcast through LinkedIn’s OAuth 2.0 authorization flow, we request the following permissions (scopes):

| Scope | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| `openid` | Authenticate you via OpenID Connect as part of the OAuth flow |
| `profile` | Access basic profile information associated with your LinkedIn account, as permitted by LinkedIn for the authorized scopes |
| `w_member_social` | Create posts on your LinkedIn profile on your behalf, only when you explicitly choose to publish |

### LinkedIn data we receive and store

| Data | When collected | Where stored |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| OAuth **access token** | During authorization and token refresh | AWS Systems Manager (SSM) Parameter Store, encrypted as a SecureString at `/quillcast/linkedin/tokens` |
| OAuth **refresh token** (if provided by LinkedIn) | During authorization and token refresh | Same SSM parameter |
| **Token expiry** timestamp | During authorization and token refresh | Same SSM parameter |
| **LinkedIn post ID** | After you publish a post | Amazon DynamoDB (as part of your draft record) |

We do **not** request or access your LinkedIn connections, messages, InMail, analytics, advertising data, or data belonging to other LinkedIn members.

Profile information obtained through the `openid` and `profile` scopes is used solely to authenticate your session and associate published content with your account. We do not build a separate profile database or use LinkedIn profile data for advertising, lead generation, recruiting, or resale.

## 3. Other Information We Process

In addition to LinkedIn OAuth data, Quillcast processes the following information **that you provide or configure** as part of normal operation:

| Data | Source | Purpose | Storage |
|------|--------|---------|---------|
| Draft post content | Generated by Amazon Bedrock (Claude) and edited by you | Review and publish to LinkedIn | DynamoDB in your AWS account |
| Topic and source metadata | RSS feeds and your configuration files | Select topics and attribute sources | DynamoDB; configuration in Amazon S3 |
| Author display settings (name, headline, profile picture URL) | Your local `.env` file | Render a local LinkedIn preview in the Streamlit UI only | Your local machine; **not sent to LinkedIn** |
| Voice and topic preferences | `config/topics.yaml` | Guide AI draft generation | Amazon S3 in your AWS account |
| Telegram bot token and chat ID (optional) | Your configuration | Send draft and publish notifications | SSM Parameter Store (if enabled) |
| Operational logs | AWS Lambda and related services | Debugging and error diagnosis | Amazon CloudWatch Logs in your AWS account |

Quillcast does **not** use cookies, web analytics, or third-party advertising trackers. The review UI runs locally on your computer and is not hosted as a public website.

---

## 4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information described above only to:

- **Authenticate** your LinkedIn account and maintain a valid OAuth session (including refreshing tokens before they expire).
- **Generate draft posts** using AI based on topics from RSS feeds or your curated list, guided by your voice and audience settings.
- **Display a local preview** of how a post may appear on LinkedIn before you publish.
- **Publish content to LinkedIn** when you explicitly click **Publish** in the local UI. **No post is published automatically** without your review and approval.
- **Record publish status** (success, failure, LinkedIn post ID, timestamps) so you can track what was published.
- **Send optional notifications** (e.g., via Telegram) about draft availability or publish results.

We do **not** use LinkedIn data or any other information for:

- Advertising or ad targeting  
- Selling or renting data to third parties  
- Lead generation, CRM enrichment, or recruiting  
- Building audience lists or profiles of other people  
- Any purpose unrelated to drafting and publishing your own content

## 6. How We Share Information

We do **not** sell, rent, or trade your personal information or LinkedIn data.

Information is shared only with the following categories of recipients, and only as needed to operate the Application in **your** environment:

| Recipient | Role | Data shared |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| **LinkedIn** | OAuth provider and publishing destination | OAuth tokens during API calls; post content you choose to publish |
| **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** | Infrastructure hosting (DynamoDB, S3, SSM, Lambda, CloudWatch, Bedrock) | All application data stored and processed in your AWS account |
| **Anthropic (via Amazon Bedrock)** | AI content generation | Topic text, voice/audience configuration, and RSS-derived context used to generate drafts — **not** your LinkedIn OAuth tokens |
| **Telegram** (optional) | Push notifications | Notification messages (e.g., draft topic, publish status) — **not** your LinkedIn OAuth tokens |

Because Quillcast is self-hosted, **you** control your AWS account and are responsible for access policies applied to that account.

## 7. Data Storage, Security, and Retention

### Storage locations

- **OAuth tokens:** AWS SSM Parameter Store (`/quillcast/linkedin/tokens`), type `SecureString`, encrypted at rest with AWS-managed keys.  
- **Drafts and publish records:** Amazon DynamoDB in your AWS account.  
- **Configuration:** Amazon S3 and local files on your computer.  
- **LinkedIn API credentials** (`LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID`, `LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET`): Stored locally in your `.env` file, which is not committed to version control.

The local Streamlit review UI **does not hold OAuth tokens**. Tokens are accessed only by the publish Lambda function in your AWS account.

### Security measures

We implement industry-standard safeguards appropriate for a self-hosted application, including:

- OAuth 2.0 with CSRF protection (`state` parameter validation)  
- Encryption of tokens at rest in SSM SecureString parameters  
- Separation of credentials: UI reads drafts; only the publish Lambda reads OAuth tokens  
- IAM-based authentication for Lambda Function URLs  
- Gitignore rules preventing secrets from being committed to source control  

You are responsible for securing your AWS account, local machine, and `.env` files.

### Retention

| Data | Retention |
|------|-----------|
| OAuth access token | Until expiry (~60 days) or until you revoke access or delete the SSM parameter |
| OAuth refresh token | Until expiry (up to ~365 days), revocation, or deletion of the SSM parameter |
| Draft and published post records | Retained in DynamoDB until you delete them; DynamoDB tables are configured to **retain** data even if infrastructure is destroyed |
| CloudWatch logs | Retained according to your AWS account’s log retention settings |

We do not retain LinkedIn profile data beyond what is necessary to operate the Application. OAuth tokens are refreshed as needed and updated in place in SSM.

## 8. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal information. Because Quillcast is self-hosted and you control your AWS account, you can exercise most rights directly:

### Revoke LinkedIn access

1. Go to [LinkedIn Settings → Permitted services](https://www.linkedin.com/psettings/permitted-services).  
2. Remove Quillcast / your LinkedIn Developer application.  
3. Delete the stored tokens from AWS SSM:  
   ```bash
   aws ssm delete-parameter --name /quillcast/linkedin/tokens
   ```

### Delete draft and publish history

Delete individual records from your DynamoDB table (`quillcast-drafts`), or delete the table entirely from your AWS account.

### Delete all Quillcast data

1. Revoke LinkedIn access (above).  
2. Delete SSM parameters under `/quillcast/`.  
3. Delete or empty the DynamoDB table and S3 config bucket in your AWS account.  
4. Remove local `.env` and configuration files from your computer.

### Re-authorize

To connect LinkedIn again after revocation, run the OAuth helper locally:

```bash
python scripts/linkedin_oauth.py
```

If you cannot complete these steps or have questions about your data, contact us using the information in Section 11.
