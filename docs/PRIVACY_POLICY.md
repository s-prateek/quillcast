# Quillcast Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** June 28, 2026  
**Last updated:** June 30, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how **Quillcast** (“the Application,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and deletes information when you connect your LinkedIn account and use the Application to draft and publish content to LinkedIn.

Quillcast is an open-source, **local-first** content pipeline. It runs on **your computer**. We do not operate a centralized service that stores your data on our servers.

---

## 1. Who This Policy Applies To

This policy applies to individuals who:

1. Install and run Quillcast on their own computer, and  
2. Authorize Quillcast to access their LinkedIn account via LinkedIn OAuth.

In this model, **you are both the user of the Application and the person whose LinkedIn account is connected**. Quillcast is designed for single-operator, personal use.

---

## 2. Information We Collect from LinkedIn

When you authorize Quillcast through LinkedIn’s OAuth 2.0 authorization flow, we request the following permissions (scopes):

| Scope | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| `openid` | Authenticate you via OpenID Connect as part of the OAuth flow |
| `profile` | Access basic profile information associated with your LinkedIn account |
| `w_member_social` | Create posts on your LinkedIn profile on your behalf, only when you explicitly choose to publish |

### LinkedIn data we receive and store

| Data | When collected | Where stored |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| OAuth **access token** | During authorization and token refresh | `data/tokens/linkedin.json` on your local machine (gitignored) |
| OAuth **refresh token** (if provided) | During authorization and token refresh | Same local file |
| **Token expiry** timestamp | During authorization and token refresh | Same local file |
| **LinkedIn post ID** | After you publish a post | `data/drafts/<post-id>.json` on your local machine |

We do **not** request or access your LinkedIn connections, messages, InMail, analytics, advertising data, or data belonging to other LinkedIn members.

---

## 3. Other Information We Process

| Data | Source | Purpose | Storage |
|------|--------|---------|---------|
| Draft post content | Generated by Claude or Gemini and edited by you | Review and publish to LinkedIn | `data/drafts/` on your local machine |
| Topic and source metadata | RSS feeds and your configuration files | Select topics and attribute sources | Draft JSON files; `config/*.yaml` |
| Author display settings | Your local `.env` file | Render a local LinkedIn preview in the Streamlit UI only | Your local machine; **not sent to LinkedIn** |
| Voice and topic preferences | `config/topics.yaml` | Guide AI draft generation | `config/topics.yaml` on your local machine |
| LLM API keys | Your `.env` file | Call Anthropic or Google for draft generation | Your local machine (gitignored) |

Quillcast does **not** use cookies, web analytics, or third-party advertising trackers.

---

## 4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information described above only to:

- **Authenticate** your LinkedIn account and maintain a valid OAuth session
- **Generate draft posts** using AI based on topics from RSS feeds or your curated list
- **Display a local preview** of how a post may appear on LinkedIn before you publish
- **Publish content to LinkedIn** when you explicitly choose to publish
- **Record publish status** so you can track what was published

We do **not** use LinkedIn data for advertising, selling data, lead generation, or any purpose unrelated to drafting and publishing your own content.

---

## 5. Legal Basis and Consent

By completing OAuth authorization, you consent to Quillcast collecting OAuth tokens and using them to publish content you approve. You may withdraw consent by revoking access in LinkedIn settings and deleting `data/tokens/linkedin.json`.

---

## 6. How We Share Information

We do **not** sell, rent, or trade your personal information or LinkedIn data.

Information is shared only with:

| Recipient | Role | Data shared |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| **LinkedIn** | OAuth provider and publishing destination | OAuth tokens during API calls; post content you choose to publish |
| **Anthropic or Google** | AI content generation (direct API) | Topic text, voice/audience configuration, and RSS-derived context — **not** your LinkedIn OAuth tokens |

Because Quillcast runs locally, **you** control your machine and all stored data.

---

## 7. Data Storage, Security, and Retention

### Storage locations

- **OAuth tokens:** `data/tokens/linkedin.json` (gitignored)
- **Drafts and publish records:** `data/drafts/*.json` (gitignored)
- **Configuration:** `config/*.yaml` (committed to repo unless you customize locally)
- **API keys:** `.env` file (gitignored)

### Security measures

- OAuth 2.0 with CSRF protection (`state` parameter validation)
- Gitignore rules preventing secrets and tokens from being committed
- You are responsible for securing your local machine and `.env` files

### Retention

| Data | Retention |
|------|-----------|
| OAuth tokens | Until expiry, revocation, or you delete `data/tokens/linkedin.json` |
| Draft records | Until you delete files in `data/drafts/` |
| API keys | Until you remove them from `.env` |

---

## 8. Your Rights and Choices

### Revoke LinkedIn access

1. Go to [LinkedIn Settings → Permitted services](https://www.linkedin.com/psettings/permitted-services)
2. Remove your LinkedIn Developer application
3. Delete `data/tokens/linkedin.json`

### Delete draft history

```bash
rm data/drafts/*.json
```

### Delete all Quillcast data

1. Revoke LinkedIn access (above)
2. Delete `data/` directory
3. Remove local `.env` file

### Re-authorize

```bash
python scripts/linkedin_oauth.py
```
